## What the SC corrected

**Stop notes 1 and 3 were not stop notes.** The first draft surfaced "strict vs. lenient reading of decision 5" and "cols-at-keypress" as questions for the SC. Both are engineering decisions, not ambiguities about what the SC wants. The bar: stop notes are for what genuinely needs the SC's input. Three-options-pick-one is not a stop note; it is the Apostle outsourcing the decision they were hired to make.

**Cols-at-keypress was a workaround.** The first draft used a setter (`setColsForKey`), a `'setColsForKey' in view` check in ViewHost, and a closure-getter alternative — three flavours of plumbing one field. The proper answer was a `TerminalState` model holding cols, rows, cursor location, and any terminal-level state views need. PrimaryView reads it like any other state. No special-case plumbing.

The relevant principle the SC named: this is a refactor. The point is improved code quality, not just the headline feature. A workaround that "works" but is shaped wrong drops the standard for every cast that follows.

## What I did with that

Added `TerminalState` (model store). Renderer pushes resize into it; ViewHost subscribes to it; PrimaryView reads `model.terminalState.cols` / `.rows` directly. The `View` interface collapses to `render(model): string[]` — cols falls out because terminalState carries it.

Decision 5 resolved as strict in the plan. `runAgent.ts` calls stores and a `flushToScroll` callback; never sees the view, the renderer, or the Screen. The SC's principle: `runAgent.ts` shouldn't hold a view reference any more than `AgentMessageHandler` should.

Audited the remaining stop notes:
- `using` declaration order — resolves organically with TerminalState (renderer pushes to a store, host subscribes to the store, no back-reference between them).
- `ConversationState.clear()` — a decision I made; showed the diff.
- `UserInput` relocation — a decision I made; named the new home (`runAgent.ts`).

The revised plan has zero stop notes. The "Stop notes" section now documents that the audit ran.

## What this taught me about the role

The Apostle's product is verified assumptions. Stop notes have a specific use: telling the SC their model didn't match the code, or surfacing genuine ambiguity about what they want. Using them as "I can see two reasonable options, you pick" is offloading specialist judgment — the thing this role exists to bring.

The cols-at-keypress workaround was a clearer failure mode. The shape `(state, cols) → string[]` looked like it forced cols to be a parameter; in fact cols was state that belonged in a store. Reaching for the parameter shape because it matched the brief's sketch is what produced the workaround. The brief's sketch was descriptive of the right idea; literalism about its parameter list was the trap.

## What didn't change

Locked decisions are still the design. EventEmitter pattern, store emissions, ViewHost coordinator, Flasher, flushSealedToScroll as a free function, AgentMessageHandler retargeting, runAgent inlining — all the same shapes. The revision is about removing workarounds and resolving punted decisions, not about reopening the design.
# 20:58 (Courier)

Thinking-toggle feature shipped. What follows is what future operators need that the code doesn't say.

## What was built

- `thinking` config object in `sdk-config.json`: `enabled` (boolean, default `true`) and `effort` (enum `low|medium|high|xhigh|max`, default `max`).
- Session-local overrides for both: thinking cycles none/on/off, effort cycles none/max/xhigh/high/medium/low. Neither writes to disk.
- `m` key in command mode opens a model-settings sub-mode. Inside: `t` cycles thinking, `e` cycles effort. ESC pops one level; Ctrl+/ exits from any depth.
- Status line: `*thinking` / `*no thinking` and `*effort:max` (only when override is set).
- SDK: `ThinkingEffort` type on `DurableConfig.thinkingEffort`, wired to `body.output_config.effort` in `RequestBuilder`.

## Traps and decisions

**`xhigh` is not in the Anthropic SDK types.** `BetaOutputConfig.effort` union is `'low' | 'medium' | 'high' | 'max' | null` — no `xhigh`, even in 0.98.0. `xhigh` is a real API value (introduced with extended thinking), the API is ahead of the types. `RequestBuilder.ts` casts at the boundary with a documented comment. The `ThinkingEffort` type in this repo correctly includes `xhigh`; the cast is only at the SDK call site. When a future SDK update adds `xhigh` to the union, the cast can be removed.

**`effort` goes on `body.output_config`, not `body.thinking`.** Effort applies to all token spend, not just thinking tokens. `BetaOutputConfig.effort` is the right field; the `thinking` block is separate.

**Callback wiring in `main.ts` must come after the closures.** `layout.setThinkingCycle(cycleThinkingOverride)` and `layout.setEffortCycle(cycleEffortOverride)` must land after the `const cycleThinkingOverride` and `const cycleEffortOverride` declarations, not immediately after `new AppLayout()`. Placing them too early is a temporal dead zone error at runtime. The calls land just before `configLoader.onChange`.

**`ThinkingEffort` must be re-exported from `packages/claude-sdk/src/index.ts`.** It is defined in `types.ts` and imported by both `main.ts` and `StatusState.ts` via `@shellicar/claude-sdk`. The Maker plan's Section 8 did not list `index.ts`; the Maker added the export as a necessary gap fill.

**Sub-mode design: no existing root bindings move.** Earlier iterations assigned `t` to thinking at the root, displacing the clipboard-text paste binding. After two failed iterations the design settled on `m` at root for a model-settings sub-mode, with `t` and `e` inside it. The rule: no existing root-level command-mode bindings may be displaced. Hold this rule for any future key-binding work.

**Root hint now shows `m` and `n`.** Before this work, `n new` was in `#handleCommandKey` but absent from the hint. The block before final Maker approval caught this. Both `m model` and `n new` are now in both root hint variants, locked by tests in `renderCommandMode.spec.ts`.

**Effort override displays when thinking is off.** The SC reviewed and accepted this during manual testing. Do not fix it without a fresh decision.

**`configLoader.onChange` needs no edit.** Resolvers re-read `overrides.*` at call time, so config reload cannot clobber the override. The existing `Object.assign(durableConfig, mapConfig())` pattern in `main.ts` is called before every turn.
