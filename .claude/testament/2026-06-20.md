The skill says add the chokidar trust-downgrade exclusion under `auditConfig.trustPolicyExclude`. That is WRONG for pnpm 11. ```yaml

  - chokidar@4.0.3

## Why the strategy changed (the spine, for whoever reads next)

claude-cli is a **library**. `pnpm audit` gates the whole resolved tree, but a library ships **declared ranges, not a lockfile** — consumers re-resolve. The 5 hono advisories are transitive (via `@modelcontextprotocol/sdk`), lockfile-only, unfixable from our manifests, and never reach consumers through anything we publish. Confirmed empirically: Dependabot alerts report all 5 against `pnpm-lock.yaml`, **zero** against any `package.json`. The only direct-dep CVE this repo ever had (`@anthropic-ai/sdk`) is already fixed. So `pnpm audit` as a blocking gate was an app-shaped tool on a library, blocking the queue over things we can't fix and don't ship. New strategy: drop the gate, rely on Dependabot **alerts** for detection (read filtered to `package.json` manifests) + **version-updates** to keep declared floors current (the one thing that actually reaches consumers).
