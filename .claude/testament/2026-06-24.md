## The finding (this is the payload)

The hypothesis (full-width line + `\n` desyncs paint) is **false under documented deferred
wrap** and **true under immediate wrap**. Under deferred, `\n`-while-pending is a single
advance that clears the flag — one row stays one physical line, no strand. Under immediate, the
last-column fill auto-advances AND the `\n` advances = two lines for one row, the skipped line
is never re-cleared → it keeps the prior frame's content = the ghost.

The bug is `paint` betting that a bare `\n` advances exactly one physical line after writing a
maybe-full-width row into an autowrap-on terminal. The bet is terminal-dependent → explains
intermittency. Fix direction (not my phase): per-row absolute positioning like
`claude-core/renderer.ts` already does, or `cols-1`, or `?7l` around the paint.

Subtlety I proved by tracing then test: the brief's "obvious" construction (static full-width
line, only input changes) does NOT reproduce — drift is identical across frames so the stranded
line is blank in both. Reproduction needs the *changing* line itself to cross the wrap boundary
(short→full-width), which is the streaming shape. Content-agnostic, not input-specific.

## Strongest open lead for whoever fixes/investigates next

Most modern terminals use deferred wrap, under which my model shows NO strand — yet the SC sees
the ghost. Best candidate: a width disagreement. `wrapLine` (via `string-width`) thinks a line
is exactly `cols`, but the terminal renders a glyph wider (emoji / ambiguous CJK / ZWJ), so the
line truly overflows → a REAL wrap on a deferred terminal too, and paint's `\n` then
over-advances. Also explains "happens less often now" (string-width improved). Worth the next
investigation.

## Build trap (cost me two runs)

The worktree had NO `dist/` for the workspace packages. `claude-core`, `claude-sdk`,
`claude-sdk-tools` all resolve via package `exports` to `dist/esm` — vitest has no src alias.
So tests and `type-check` both fail with "Cannot find module '@shellicar/...'" until you
`pnpm --filter <pkg> build` them. My three files had ZERO errors once deps were built;
type-check exit 0. Don't trust `TsDiagnostics` here either — it used its own tsconfig and cried
about `#private` and `Intl.Segmenter` (both used all over the real codebase). Direct
`tsc -p tsconfig.check.json` is the truth.
